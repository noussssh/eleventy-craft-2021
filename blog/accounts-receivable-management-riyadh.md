## The Receivables Challenge in Saudi Arabia

Saudi Arabia's business culture has its own rhythms. Payment terms can stretch longer than expected. Following up feels awkward. And tracking who owes what across dozens or hundreds of customers? That's where things get complicated.

Companies in Riyadh, Jeddah, and Dammam often struggle with:

- **Visibility**: Who owes money, and for how long?
- **Follow-up**: When was the last contact with each customer?
- **Aging**: Which receivables are at risk?
- **Reconciliation**: Do customer records match yours?

## ERPNext: Your Receivables Command Center

### Complete Customer View

Every interaction with a customer in one place:

### Aging Analysis That Drives Action

ERPNext's aging reports categorize receivables by how long they've been outstanding:

- Current (not yet due)
- 1-30 days overdue
- 31-60 days overdue
- 61-90 days overdue
- Over 90 days overdue

Each category demands different action. Fresh receivables need gentle reminders. Older ones might need escalation. ERPNext helps you prioritize.

Stop wasting time sending manual follow-up emails. ERPNext can automatically:
