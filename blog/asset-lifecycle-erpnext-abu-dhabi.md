A company in Dubai buys a vehicle. It's registered in the system, assigned to a department, maintained regularly, and eventually sold when too old to operate efficiently. This journey—from purchase to disposal—is the asset lifecycle.

For Dubai businesses with significant asset investments, lifecycle management optimizes value at every stage.

## The Asset Lifecycle

### Stage 1: Planning

Before acquisition:

- Need identification
- Requirements definition
- Budget allocation
- Approval process

### Stage 2: Acquisition

Getting the asset:

- Procurement process
- Vendor selection
- Purchase execution
- Delivery and acceptance

### Stage 3: Deployment

Putting to use:

- Asset registration
- Location assignment
- Custodian allocation
- System configuration

### Stage 4: Operation

Using the asset:

- Daily utilization
- Performance monitoring
- Issue identification
- Value generation

### Stage 5: Maintenance

Keeping it working:

- Preventive service
- Corrective repairs
- Upgrades
- Optimization

### Stage 6: Disposal

End of life:

- Retirement decision
- Disposal method
- Sale or scrap
- Record closure

## Lifecycle Decisions

### Buy vs. Lease

Acquisition choice:

**Buy Considerations**
- Long-term need
- Control importance
- Tax benefits
- Capital availability

**Lease Considerations**
- Short-term need
- Technology change
- Cash preservation
- Flexibility

### Repair vs. Replace

Maintenance decision:

**Repair When**
- Cost effective
- Life remaining
- Parts available
- Downtime acceptable

**Replace When**
- Repair costly
- Obsolete
- Unreliable
- Better options exist

### Upgrade vs. Replace

Enhancement decision:

**Upgrade When**
- Core is good
- Cost effective
- Extended life
- Meets needs

**Replace When**
- Fundamental limitation
- Technology gap
- Total cost favors
- Strategic change
