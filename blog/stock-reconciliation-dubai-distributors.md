The numbers in your system say 500 units. The physical count shows 473. Where did 27 units go?

This scenario plays out in warehouses across Dubai every day. The question isn't whether discrepancies exist - they always do. The question is how you find them, fix them, and prevent recurrence.

## Why Stock Differs from Records

Inventory discrepancies have many causes:

**Receiving Errors**
- Counted wrong when goods arrived
- Quantity mismatch not caught
- Damaged items not recorded

**Picking Errors**
- Picked wrong quantity
- Picked wrong item
- Not recorded in system

**Shipping Errors**
- Loaded wrong amounts
- Loaded wrong items
- Paperwork doesn't match physical

**Theft and Shrinkage**
- Internal theft
- External theft
- Unexplained losses

**Administrative Errors**
- Data entry mistakes
- Timing differences
- Wrong locations recorded

**Physical Loss**
- Damage in warehouse
- Expiry and disposal
- Quality rejections not recorded

## Reconciliation Methods

Different approaches suit different businesses:

### Full Physical Count

Count everything at once:

**Advantages**
- Complete accuracy verification
- Catches all discrepancies
- Clean restart

**Disadvantages**
- Disrupts operations
- Labor intensive
- Discrepancies aged since last count

Best for: Annual verification, audit preparation

### Cycle Counting

Count portion of inventory continuously:

**Common approaches**
- ABC classification (count A items monthly, B quarterly, C annually)
- Random selection daily/weekly
- Location-based rotation

**Advantages**
- Less operational disruption
- Earlier discrepancy detection
- Continuous accuracy maintenance

**Disadvantages**
- Requires ongoing discipline
- Full picture takes time to build

Best for: Ongoing accuracy management

### Perpetual Verification

Count items at transaction points:

- Verify during picking
- Count during receiving
- Check during transfers

**Advantages**
- Integrates with normal work
- Immediate discrepancy detection
- No separate counting exercise

Best for: High-accuracy operations

## Analysis After Reconciliation

Adjustments alone don't solve problems. Analysis does:

### Variance Reports

Review adjustments:

- Which items have largest variances?
- Which warehouses have problems?
- Which variance types dominate (shortage vs. overage)?
- Trends over time?

### Root Cause Investigation

For significant variances:

- When might the discrepancy have occurred?
- Who was involved in transactions?
- What processes touched the items?
- System issues or human issues?

### Process Improvement

Based on findings:

- Strengthen receiving verification
- Improve picking accuracy
- Enhance security measures
- Add system controls

## Best Practices for Dubai Distributors

### Count Preparation

Before counting:
- Freeze stock movements if possible
- Organize counting teams
- Prepare count sheets or mobile devices
- Clear staging areas

### Count Execution

During counting:
- Count twice before recording
- Supervisor verification of counts
- Note damaged or questionable items
- Document count conditions

### Post-Count Processing

After counting:
- Enter results promptly
- Review large variances before posting
- Document explanations
- Approve and post adjustments

### Continuous Improvement

Ongoing:
- Track accuracy metrics
- Reduce variance rates
- Celebrate improvements
- Address problem areas
