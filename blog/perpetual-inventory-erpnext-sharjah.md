The old way: Stop operations, count everything, value the inventory, adjust the books. Repeat monthly, quarterly, or annually. Disruption, inaccuracy, and delayed information.

The modern way: Every stock movement automatically updates both inventory records and accounting entries. Real-time accuracy without operational disruption.

This is perpetual inventory, and it's transforming how Dubai businesses manage stock.

## Understanding Perpetual Inventory

In a perpetual inventory system:

**Every Receipt**
- Increases inventory quantity
- Increases inventory value in accounts
- Records cost basis for future sales

**Every Issue**
- Decreases inventory quantity
- Decreases inventory value in accounts
- Records cost of goods sold

**At Any Moment**
- Stock records match accounting records
- Current valuation available instantly
- No waiting for physical counts to update books

## The Alternative: Periodic Inventory

Some businesses still use periodic systems:

- Count inventory at period end
- Compare to last count
- Calculate cost of goods sold by formula

**Problems with periodic**:
- No real-time visibility
- Shrinkage hidden until count
- Cannot track profitability between counts
- Physical counting disrupts operations
- Errors accumulate between counts

## ERPNext Perpetual Inventory

ERPNext implements perpetual inventory seamlessly.

### Automatic Accounting

When you create a purchase receipt:
- Inventory account debited
- Accounts payable credited
- No manual journal entries needed

When you create a delivery note:
- Cost of goods sold debited
- Inventory account credited
- Automatic based on valuation method

### Account Configuration

Set up accounts in ERPNext:

**Stock In Hand** (Asset account)
- Default for inventory value
- Separate by warehouse if needed

**Cost of Goods Sold** (Expense account)
- Captures cost when items sell
- Enables gross margin calculation

**Stock Received But Not Billed** (Liability)
- Handles timing differences

**Stock Adjustment** (Expense)
- For count discrepancies

### Valuation Methods

Perpetual inventory works with any valuation method:

- FIFO: Issues at oldest purchase price
- Moving Average: Issues at average cost
- Perpetual tracking maintains accuracy for both

### Real-Time Reports

Because inventory and accounting sync continuously:

**Balance Sheet**: Accurate inventory asset value anytime
**Profit and Loss**: Correct cost of goods sold in real-time
**Gross Margin**: Calculate profitability by sale, customer, or product
**Stock Reports**: Match financial records perfectly

## Making Perpetual Inventory Work

Perpetual inventory requires discipline:

### Record Every Movement

- Goods received? Create purchase receipt
- Goods shipped? Create delivery note
- Internal transfer? Create stock transfer
- Damaged goods? Create stock entry

Informal movements break the system.

### Verify Accuracy

Perpetual doesn't eliminate counting. It changes counting's purpose:

- Cycle counting catches discrepancies early
- Spot checks verify process compliance
- Annual counts confirm system accuracy

Discrepancies require adjustment entries.

### Train Your Team

Everyone handling stock must understand:

- Transactions record before physical movement
- Proper documentation requirements
- Error correction procedures
- The importance of accuracy

### Monitor Variances

Review adjustment entries:

- What discrepancies are occurring?
- Which warehouses have problems?
- What items have issues?
- Root cause analysis and correction
