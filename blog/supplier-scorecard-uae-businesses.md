"They're a good supplier" isn't good enough anymore. In the UAE's competitive business environment, you need to know exactly how good - and how your suppliers compare to each other.

Supplier scorecards transform subjective impressions into objective measurements. For businesses across Dubai, Dubai, and the Emirates, systematic performance measurement is essential for supply chain excellence.

**Accountability**

- Discussion grounded
- Improvement expected

**Decision Support**
- Sourcing decisions informed
- Volume allocation rational
- Development priorities clear
- Exit decisions justified

**Improvement**
- Trends visible
- Root causes identifiable
- Progress trackable
- Success recognizable

## Implementation Approach

### Define Your Criteria

What matters most:

- Industry-specific factors
- Company priorities
- Customer requirements
- Strategic objectives

### Set Weights

Importance varies:

- Quality might be 40%
- Delivery might be 30%
- Cost might be 20%
- Service might be 10%

Adjust for your situation.

### Establish Scoring Scales

Clear definitions:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Excellent - Exceeds expectations |
| 4 | Good - Meets expectations consistently |
| 3 | Acceptable - Meets minimum requirements |
| 2 | Poor - Below expectations |
| 1 | Unacceptable - Fails requirements |

### Capture Data

Systematic collection:

- Transaction data automatic
- Inspection results linked
- Delivery data captured
- Issue tracking integrated

### Calculate Regularly

Consistent periods:

- Monthly for active suppliers
- Quarterly for periodic suppliers
- Annual summary reviews

## Common Pitfalls

### Too Many Metrics

Keep it manageable:

- Focus on what matters
- 5-10 key metrics
- Avoid measurement for its own sake
- Keep it actionable

### Inconsistent Application

Apply fairly:

- Same criteria for similar suppliers
- Consistent data collection
- Regular calculation
- Equal treatment

### Ignoring Results

Use the data:

- Review regularly
- Take action
- Communicate
- Drive improvement

### All Stick, No Carrot

Balance approach:

- Recognize excellence
- Not just punishment
- Reward improvement
- Build partnerships
